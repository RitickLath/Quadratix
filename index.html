What is Quadratic?

Quadratics can be defined as a polynomial equation of a second degree, which implies that it comprises a minimum of one term that is squared. It is also called quadratic equations. The general form of the quadratic equation is: ax² + bx + c = 0 where x is an unknown variable and a, b, c are numerical coefficients. For example, x² + 2x +1 is a quadratic or quadratic equation. Here, a ≠ 0 because if it equals zero then the equation will not remain quadratic anymore and it will become a linear equation, such as: bx+c=0 Thus, this equation cannot be called a quadratic equation.The terms a, b and c are also called quadratic coefficients. The solutions to the quadratic equation are the values of the unknown variable x, which satisfy the equation. These solutions are called roots or zeros of quadratic equations. The roots of any polynomial are the solutions for the given equation.

How to solve Quadratic?

Quadratic equations are a fundamental part of algebra and have many real-world applications.
They are equations of the form: ax2 + bx+c=0

Where,
a, b, and c are constants,
x is the variable we want to solve for.


  • Identify the Coefficients:


    Before we can solve the quadratic equation, we need to identify the values of a, b and c.
    These coefficients will determine our approach to solving the equation.

    • a: Coefficient of x2.
    • b: Coefficient of x.
    • c: Constant.

  • Calculate the Discriminant:


    The discriminant, denoted by Δ, is a key value that helps us determine the nature of the solutions:
    Δ = b 2 - 4ac

    The discriminant can be one of three types:

    • Δ > 0 : Two distinct real solutions.
    • Δ = 0 : One real solution (a repeated root).
    • Δ < 0 : No real solutions (complex roots).

  • Apply the Quadratic Formula:


    The quadratic formula is:

    \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \)


    To find the solutions for x, we can use the quadratic formula:
    • Δ > 0, there are two real solutions. The ± sign indicates that there are two solutions, one using the plus sign and the other using the minus sign.
    • Δ = 0, there's one real solution (a repeated root).
    • Δ < 0, there are no real solutions, but there are complex roots.
